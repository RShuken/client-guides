What P Is

Your personal AI -- everywhere you are

P is a private AI assistant configured specifically for you. It answers you on Telegram, thinks with cloud-grade reasoning when it helps, falls back to a local model on your laptop when it doesn't need the cloud, and remembers what you share with it -- in a knowledge base that never leaves your machine.

Chat

Brain

Two brains, one assistant

KB

Private knowledge base

Drop a link, P saves it. Ask a question later, P searches your saved content with real semantic understanding -- all local, no API calls.

Lock

How It Works

Three layers working together

Every message goes through a router that picks the right brain for the job. Fast answers from the local model. Hard reasoning from the cloud model. Grounded answers from your knowledge base.

C

Primary brain: GPT-5.4 via ChatGPT

For anything that needs real reasoning -- planning, writing, code, complex questions. Uses your ChatGPT subscription (no separate API key). Only kicks in when the question actually needs it.

Cloud
G

Fallback brain: Gemma 4 on your laptop

Google's open-weight model, running entirely on your ThinkPad's CPU at ~20-40 tokens per second. Fires when the cloud is unavailable, or when you want a quick local answer with zero credits burned.

Local
K

Your knowledge base: semantic search over saved content

Drop a URL, P reads it, embeds it with nomic-embed-text (also local), and stores it. Later, ask a question -- P searches your saved content by meaning, not keywords, and answers with citations.

Local KB
Your Private KB

Save anything. Ask anything later.

The knowledge base is yours -- stored as a plain file on your laptop. No cloud, no account, no API key. Just text and vectors.

URL

Ingest a URL

Send P a link. It fetches the page, strips the HTML, chunks the text into 500-word windows, embeds each one, and files it away. Takes ~3 seconds for a typical article.

Search

Semantic search

Ask "what did I save about X" -- P embeds your question, finds the closest chunks in your KB by meaning, and surfaces them. Not keyword matching -- real conceptual similarity.

RAG

Grounded answers

Ask P a question, it retrieves the top-K relevant chunks, hands them to Gemma as context, and generates an answer with numbered citations back to the source URLs. If the retrieved content doesn't have the answer, P says so instead of making something up.

Where it lives: C:\Users\paul\.openclaw\workspace\kb\. A single file (store.jsonl) with one chunk per line. You can open it in any text editor, copy it, back it up, or move it to another machine -- it's yours.

Privacy Model

What stays on your machine. What leaves.

No dark patterns. Here's exactly where your data goes.

Stays on your ThinkPad

  • Your identity -- how P talks to you, what it knows about you
  • Your knowledge base -- everything you've saved or ingested
  • All embeddings (the numeric representations of your content)
  • The local Gemma 4 model and its inference
  • Conversation memory and context from past chats
  • Logs of what P did (for your own audit)
  • Any file or workspace content P touches

Goes to the cloud (only when used)

  • Telegram messages -- Telegram's infrastructure, same as any chat with anyone
  • ChatGPT inference calls -- your prompt + any context you allow, to OpenAI (same as a normal ChatGPT conversation)
  • URL fetches when you ingest a page (P goes and reads the URL)

The important nuance: P uses GPT-5.4 only when it thinks the question benefits from it. Simple lookups, KB retrieval, and anything embedded-based use the local model. If you're worried about sending something to the cloud, tell P "answer locally" or "keep this private" -- it understands and will stay on the Gemma path.

What P Can Do

Capabilities and honest limits

Two lists. One of things P handles well. One of things it won't pretend to handle.

What P does today

  • Answer questions with cloud-grade reasoning (GPT-5.4)
  • Answer questions locally when the cloud isn't needed (Gemma 4)
  • Save links and articles for later recall
  • Search your saved content by meaning
  • Summarize saved content with citations
  • Strip AI-sounding prose from writing (humanizer skill)
  • Respect Windows-specific approval gates for risky actions
  • Run 24/7 in the background -- no terminal needed
  • Respond on your phone via Telegram, anywhere you have signal

What P doesn't do (yet)

  • Access your email, calendar, or Google Workspace (pending)
  • PDF ingestion into the KB (text/HTML only for now)
  • Image generation (no Gemini key configured)
  • Read your screen or click things for you
  • Watch your files and react automatically
  • Make phone calls, send SMS, or post to social media
  • Know what happened outside its chat history (unless you ingested it)
  • Promise it won't ever be wrong -- always verify anything important

Questions

Frequently asked

Does P cost me anything per message?
Not directly. You already pay for ChatGPT; P uses that subscription's included Codex credits for cloud-brain calls. The local Gemma model is free forever. Telegram is free. So the marginal cost of most messages is zero. The only way you'd incur extra spend is if you hooked P up to a paid API (we haven't done that yet).
What happens if my ChatGPT credits run out?
P automatically falls back to Gemma 4 on your laptop. You'll notice answers get a little slower and a little less polished, but nothing breaks. When your credits reset (next billing period), P goes back to using the cloud brain for hard questions.
What if my laptop is off?
Then P is off. Everything lives on your ThinkPad -- both the local model and the knowledge base. Telegram will still accept your message and queue it, but P can't respond until the laptop is awake. This is the main tradeoff of a private, on-device setup.
Is my knowledge base really private?
Yes. The file lives at C:\Users\paul\.openclaw\workspace\kb\store.jsonl. Embeddings are computed locally by nomic-embed-text. Searches happen locally. Nothing from your KB leaves your machine -- unless you explicitly ask P to include a saved snippet in a cloud-brain answer, in which case that snippet goes to the cloud brain along with your question (just like if you pasted it into ChatGPT yourself).
Can I change P's name or personality?
Yes. P's current identity is a placeholder ("lobster energy, sardonic, direct"). Next time we chat, we can swap the name, emoji, tone, humor level, and no-go topics to whatever you want. The identity lives in a single text file P reads every conversation -- easy to edit.
How do I back up the KB?
Copy C:\Users\paul\.openclaw\workspace\kb\ anywhere you want. It's just plain JSONL -- portable to any other machine, greppable, inspectable. If you want automatic backups, we can set up a scheduled task to sync it to OneDrive, a USB drive, or wherever.
What if P says something wrong?
Tell P. It'll correct itself and remember the correction. More broadly: P isn't perfect. For anything that matters (medical, financial, legal), verify the answer against an authoritative source before acting on it. P should already hedge on uncertain topics; if it's overconfident, push back.
Who has access to this bot?
Only you. The bot is configured with a Telegram allowlist containing exactly one user ID -- yours. If anyone else messages @PauloradoBot, it silently ignores them. If you want to add someone (say, a trusted collaborator), we can add their Telegram ID to the allowlist.
How do I reach Ryan if something breaks?
Same way we've been talking -- drop him a message. If P itself is broken, you can also just tell @PauloradoBot what's wrong -- it'll log the symptom, and Ryan has remote access to investigate.
What's next?
A few things we haven't done yet but can: personalize P (custom name, tone, no-go topics), add Google Workspace integration, hook up a daily briefing, set up calendar/email awareness, add more skills as you identify specific needs. Nothing is required -- the current setup is complete and useful on its own.
